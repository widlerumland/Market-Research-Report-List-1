<p><p>On-board computers (OBCs) are critical components in aerospace, automotive, and other high-tech industries, serving as the central processing unit for various functions. The market for OBCs is divided into volatile and non-volatile memory segments. Volatile memory, such as RAM, temporarily stores data for quick access and processing, but requires constant power. In contrast, non-volatile memory, like flash storage, retains information even when powered off, making it essential for long-term data storage and reliability in mission-critical applications.</p></p>
