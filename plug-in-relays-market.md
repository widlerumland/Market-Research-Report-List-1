<p><p>The plug-in relay market is broadly categorized into DC and AC relays, serving different applications. DC relays are designed to operate with direct current, commonly used in low-voltage applications such as automotive systems and control circuits. In contrast, AC relays function with alternating current, making them ideal for higher voltage applications like HVAC systems and industrial machinery. Both types offer advantages like easy replacement and servicing, enhancing their appeal in automation, telecommunications, and electrical distribution sectors.</p></p>
