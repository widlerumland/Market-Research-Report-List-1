<p><p>Grain drying systems are essential for preserving harvested crops, and they come in two primary types: stationary and mobile grain dryers. Stationary grain dryers are fixed installations, often used on farms or commercial facilities, designed for high-capacity drying with consistent efficiency. In contrast, mobile grain dryers offer flexibility and portability, allowing operators to dry grain on-site or transport it between locations. Both types cater to different operational needs, optimizing grain storage and reducing moisture content to prevent spoilage.</p></p>
